## A/B Testing Ideas

**Subject Lines:**
- Question format vs. statement
- Emoji vs. no emoji
- Short (< 40 chars) vs. long (> 50 chars)
- Benefit-focused vs. curiosity-focused

**CTAs:**
- "Try it now" vs. "Start organizing" vs. "Get started free"
- Button vs. text link
- Single CTA vs. multiple CTAs
- Top placement vs. bottom placement

**Email Length:**
- Short (< 200 words) vs. long (> 500 words)
- Story-based vs. feature-list
- Personal vs. professional tone
- Formal vs. casual language

---

## Email Design Guidelines

**Keep it simple:**
- Plain text or very minimal HTML
- Mobile-first design
- Large, tappable buttons
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)

**Visual hierarchy:**
- Clear subject line
- Compelling preview text
- Scannable headings
- Obvious CTA

**Brand consistency:**
- Purple accent color (#6c5ce7)
- Clean, modern font
- Consistent voice
- Professional but friendly

## Legal Requirements

**Include in every email:**
- Physical mailing address
- Unsubscribe link (one-click)
- Privacy policy link
- Company name
- "Why you're receiving this email" message

**GDPR compliance:**
- Double opt-in for EU users
- Clear data usage explanation
- Easy data deletion
- No sharing with third parties statement

**CAN-SPAM compliance:**
- Accurate "From" name
- Relevant subject line
- Identify as advertisement (if applicable)
- Honor unsubscribe within 10 days
